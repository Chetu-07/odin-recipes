Rajma Chawal Recipe


Rajma Chawal is a classic North Indian dish made with kidney beans (rajma) cooked in a rich tomato-based gravy, served with steamed rice.

Ingredients

For Rajma (Kidney Bean Curry)

  • 1 cup dried rajma (kidney beans) or 2 cups canned rajma
  • 2 tablespoons oil or ghee
  • 1 teaspoon cumin seeds
  • 1 bay leaf
  • 1-inch cinnamon stick
  • 1 onion (finely chopped)
  • 1 teaspoon ginger-garlic paste
  • 2 tomatoes (pureed)
  • 1 green chili (chopped, optional)
  • 1 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1 teaspoon red chili powder
  • 1 teaspoon coriander powder
  • 1 teaspoon garam masala
  • 1 teaspoon rajma masala (optional)
  • Salt to taste
  • 1 teaspoon dry mango powder (amchur) or lemon juice
  • 2 tablespoons chopped coriander leaves
  • 3 cups water

For Chawal (Rice)

  • 1 cup basmati rice
  • 2 cups water
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1 teaspoon oil or ghee (optional)




Instructions

Preparing Rajma (Kidney Bean Curry)

  1. Soak rajma overnight in water. If using canned rajma, rinse and drain.
  2. Boil rajma in a pressure cooker with 3 cups of water and salt for 5-6 whistles or until soft.
  3. Heat oil or ghee in a pan. Add cumin seeds, bay leaf, and cinnamon stick. SautÃ© for 30 seconds.
  4. Add chopped onions and sautÃ© until golden brown.
  5. Stir in ginger-garlic paste and cook until fragrant.
  6. Add tomato puree and green chili. Cook until oil separates from the mixture.
  7. Mix in turmeric, red chili powder, coriander powder, and rajma masala.
  8. Combine the boiled rajma with its water into the pan. Mash some beans for a thicker consistency.
  9. Simmer for 10-15 minutes to enhance the flavors.
  10. Add garam masala and dry mango powder (or lemon juice). Stir well.
  11. Garnish with fresh coriander leaves and turn off the heat.

Preparing Chawal (Rice)

  1. Rinse basmati rice under running water until the water runs clear.
  2. In a pot, add rice, water, salt, and oil/ghee (if using).
  3. Bring to a boil, then reduce heat to low. Cover and simmer for 10-12 minutes.
  4. Once cooked, fluff the rice with a fork and let it rest for 5 minutes.
