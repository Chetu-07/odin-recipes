Chole Bhature Recipe


Chole Bhature is a popular North Indian dish consisting of spicy chickpea curry (chole) served with deep-fried fluffy bread (bhature).

Ingredients

For Chole (Chickpea Curry)**

  • 1 cup dried chickpeas (or 2 cups canned chickpeas)
  • 2 tablespoons oil
  • 1 teaspoon cumin seeds
  • 1 bay leaf
  • 1-inch cinnamon stick
  • 2 cloves
  • 1 black cardamom
  • 1 onion (finely chopped)
  • 1 teaspoon ginger-garlic paste
  • 2 tomatoes (pureed)
  • 1 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1 teaspoon red chili powder
  • 1 teaspoon coriander powder
  • 1 teaspoon garam masala
  • 1 teaspoon chole masala (optional)
  • Salt to taste
  • 1 teaspoon dry mango powder (amchur) or lemon juice
  • 2 tablespoons chopped coriander leaves

## **Instructions**

### **Preparing Chole (Chickpea Curry)**

  1. Soak Chickpeas : Soak dried chickpeas overnight in water. If using canned chickpeas, rinse and drain.
  2. Boil Chickpeas :In a pressure cooker, add soaked chickpeas, 3 cups of water, and salt. Cook for 5-6 whistles or until soft. If using a pot, boil until tender (about 40-50 minutes).
  3. Tempering : Heat oil in a pan. Add cumin seeds, bay leaf, cinnamon stick, cloves, and black cardamom. SautÃ© for 30 seconds.
  4. Onion & Tomato Base : Add chopped onions and sautÃ© until golden brown. Stir in ginger-garlic paste and cook for 1 minute. Add tomato puree and cook until oil separates.
  5. Spices : Add turmeric, red chili powder, coriander powder, and chole masala. Cook for a minute.
  6. Combine : Add the boiled chickpeas along with the water they were cooked in. Mash some chickpeas for thickness. Simmer for 10 minutes.
  7. Finishing Touch : Add garam masala and dry mango powder (or lemon juice). Mix well. Garnish with chopped coriander.
